Our Mission & Maxims

Six beliefs that
drive everything we do.

DREAM's mission fuels our "why," serving as the overarching purpose of our work. Our maxims build on it — they're the core beliefs that guide everyday practice, from the kindergarten cafeteria to the senior portfolio defense.

01

All kids can.
This kid can.

We hold confidence in every child's capacity — especially when broader society limits opportunity. We tailor support to meet individual needs, equipping students with the resources to turn aspirations into achievements.

02

DREAM is family.

Beyond our roles as students, educators, or staff, we form a unified community. We care for one another, especially during hardship, knowing that student success requires comprehensive support systems.

03

Teamwork makes the DREAM work.

Collaboration and mutual inspiration drive progress. We value collective accomplishment, understanding that sustained advancement comes through coordinated effort.

04

Fun is a serious value.

Work combines dedication with enjoyment. Our roots in youth recreation keep us celebrating achievements of all sizes — and sustaining the joy in our efforts.

05

DREAM big.

We embrace ambitious vision and corresponding belief. From a small community initiative to a network serving thousands, our story shows what dreaming big can do.

06

Fail. Persist. Exceed.

Excellence is a continuous journey. Drawing from baseball, we teach resilience through setbacks, learning from failures, and progressively raising performance standards.

What's next · Alumni programming

Once a Legend,
always a Legend.

DREAM alumni are called Legends. The Legends Fellows program — established in 2011 — provides graduates six guaranteed years of post-secondary support, with college coaching, near-peer mentorship, and summer bridge built in. By 2030, we'll serve nearly 1,000 Legends.

Flagship program

Legends Fellows

Each Legend gets a dedicated Program Coordinator, a 4-week summer bridge before freshman year, plus career workshops, networking events, internships, and mentorship throughout college.

  • 1:1 coaching from a dedicated Program Coordinator
  • Summer bridge program for first-year students
  • Career workshops, conferences & internships
  • Campus visits, care packages, alumni reunions
Career pathways

Upward Pathways

For graduates pursuing careers without a Bachelor's degree — training, certification, mentorship, case management, and job placement support.

Student voice

Alumni Council

Recently established to represent program participants and graduates across three decades — Council members shape organizational strategy and engage the broader Legends network.

Building AI Literacy Through History: aiEDU + DREAM Charter Schools

aiEDU is partnering with DREAM Charter Schools to integrate AI literacy into middle-school social studies classrooms in East Harlem and the Bronx — embedding AI concepts within history rather than as a stand-alone subject. The collaboration was sparked by Dionna Rogers, a DREAM Lead Teacher and AI Champion, with a focus on critical thinking and reaching students often left out of tech conversations.
